Orthopedic Surgery — Fractures, Ligaments & Joint Care

From a fall, an accident, or wear over time — broken bones and torn ligaments need fast, accurate diagnosis and skilled surgical repair.

Conditions We Treat

  • Limb fractures from falls, road accidents, or trauma
  • Cranial cruciate ligament (CCL) tears — the most common cause of sudden rear-leg lameness in dogs
  • Patellar luxation (kneecap slipping out of place), common in small breeds
  • Hip dysplasia and joint degeneration in older pets
  • Dislocations following trauma

Why Imaging Comes First

We never operate on a guess. Digital X-ray gives us a clear view of bone alignment and fracture pattern same-visit. For complex cases involving soft tissue or subtle joint damage, our in-house CT scan provides the cross-sectional detail needed to plan surgery precisely — without sending you to an outside imaging center and waiting days for results.

Surgical Approach

Depending on the injury, repair may involve pinning, plating, external fixation, or ligament reconstruction. All orthopedic procedures are done under gas anaesthesia with full vital monitoring. Recovery includes pain management and a structured activity-restriction plan — we'll walk you through exactly what your pet can and can't do during healing.

Common Signs to Watch For

Sudden limping or refusing to bear weight
Visible swelling around a joint or limb
Yelping when a leg is touched
Limb held at an unusual angle after a fall
Gradual worsening lameness in older pets

Frequently Asked Questions

My dog suddenly won't put weight on a back leg — is this an emergency?
Sudden non-weight-bearing lameness, especially after exercise, often points to a cruciate ligament tear. It's not always a same-hour emergency, but should be seen within a day or two — call us and describe what you're seeing.
Can small fractures heal without surgery?
Some hairline fractures in small bones can be managed with strict rest and splinting. We'll tell you honestly whether your pet's specific fracture needs surgical fixation based on the X-ray.
How long is recovery from ligament surgery?
Typically 8-12 weeks of restricted activity with structured rehabilitation. We provide a detailed week-by-week recovery plan.
Do you have CT scan on-site?
Yes — for complex fractures or joint cases where X-ray alone isn't enough detail, we have in-house CT capability, so there's no referral wait.
