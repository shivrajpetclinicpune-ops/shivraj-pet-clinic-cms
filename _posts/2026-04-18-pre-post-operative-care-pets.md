<h2>Our Commitment to Surgical Safety</h2><p>At Shivraj Pet Clinic we have performed over 200 surgical procedures on exotic and companion animals. Our protocol prioritises safety at every step.</p><div class='post-highlight'>🏥 We use gas anaesthesia (isoflurane) for all surgeries — the same standard used in human hospitals. Significantly safer than injectable-only anaesthesia.</div><h2>Pre-operative Preparation</h2><h3>Dogs and Cats:</h3><ul><li><strong>Fast for 8–12 hours</strong> before surgery — no food after midnight</li><li><strong>Water is fine</strong> up to 2 hours before</li><li><strong>Pre-surgical blood work</strong> — we run CBC and biochemistry</li><li>Do NOT give medications on the morning of surgery unless instructed</li></ul><h3>Exotic Animals (Rabbits, Guinea Pigs, Birds):</h3><ul><li><strong>Do NOT fast rabbits or guinea pigs</strong> — their digestive system must keep moving</li></ul><div class='post-warning'>⚠️ Most dangerous mistake: fasting a rabbit before surgery. Always tell us if another vet recommended this.</div><h2>Post-operative Care at Home</h2><ul><li><strong>Rest for 24–48 hours</strong> — no running, jumping or stairs</li><li><strong>E-collar (cone)</strong> — must be worn to prevent licking the wound</li><li><strong>Wound care:</strong> Keep dry, check daily for redness or discharge</li><li><strong>Medications:</strong> Give exactly as prescribed — never skip pain relief</li><li><strong>Follow-up:</strong> Suture removal 10–14 days after surgery</li></ul>
