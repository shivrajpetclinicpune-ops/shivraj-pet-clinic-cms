<h2>What is Tick Fever?</h2><p>Tick fever is a group of infections transmitted by tick bites. In Pune and PCMC we primarily see Ehrlichiosis and Babesiosis — both transmitted by the Brown Dog Tick, extremely common in green localities.</p><div class='post-highlight'>📍 High-risk areas in Pune: Pimple Nilakh, Wakad, Aundh, Baner, Pashan. If your dog goes outdoors, tick fever testing is essential twice a year.</div><h2>Symptoms to Watch For</h2><ul><li><strong>Sudden lethargy</strong> — energetic yesterday, barely moving today</li><li><strong>Complete loss of appetite</strong></li><li><strong>High fever</strong> — above 103°F (39.4°C)</li><li><strong>Pale or yellow gums</strong> — sign of anaemia</li><li><strong>Blood in urine</strong> — dark brown or red (classic Babesiosis sign)</li><li><strong>Bleeding from nose or gums</strong> — advanced Ehrlichiosis</li></ul><h2>Diagnosis and Treatment</h2><p>We run CBC and tick fever panel in our in-house lab — results in 1–2 hours. Ehrlichiosis: Doxycycline for 4 weeks. Babesiosis: Imidocarb injections. Most dogs recover fully when caught early.</p><div class='post-warning'>⚠️ Do not wait. Tick fever progresses from mild to critical in 48–72 hours. Call +91 77569 65169 immediately if your dog is lethargic and not eating.</div><h2>Prevention</h2><ul><li>Monthly tick prevention — Bravecto, NexGard or Simparica</li><li>Check your dog for ticks after every outdoor walk</li><li>Annual tick fever blood test even without symptoms</li></ul>
