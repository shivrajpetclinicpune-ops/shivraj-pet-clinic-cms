<h2>What is Canine Gastroenteritis?</h2><p>The most dangerous cause is Canine Parvovirus (CPV) — a highly contagious viral disease that kills rapidly, especially in unvaccinated puppies.</p><div class='post-highlight'>⚠️ Parvovirus survives in soil for up to 1 year. A puppy does not need to meet an infected dog to catch it — contaminated shoes, hands or grass are enough.</div><h2>Signs of Parvovirus</h2><ul><li><strong>Severe, bloody diarrhoea</strong> — often the first visible sign</li><li><strong>Profuse vomiting</strong> — cannot keep water down</li><li><strong>Extreme lethargy</strong> — puppy cannot stand or lift head</li><li><strong>Complete loss of appetite</strong></li></ul><h2>Vaccination Protocol</h2><ul><li><strong>6 weeks:</strong> DHPPi — Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvovirus, Parainfluenza</li><li><strong>9 weeks:</strong> DHPPi + Leptospirosis booster</li><li><strong>12 weeks:</strong> DHPPi + Rabies</li><li><strong>16 weeks:</strong> Final puppy booster + Kennel Cough</li><li><strong>Annual:</strong> Booster + Rabies</li></ul><div class='post-warning'>⚠️ Do not take your unvaccinated puppy to dog parks, pet shops or public areas. Until the 12-week vaccine is complete, carry your puppy — do not let it walk on public ground.</div><h2>Treatment if Infected</h2><p>Intensive supportive care — IV fluids, anti-nausea medication, antibiotics. Survival with early treatment: 80–95%. Without treatment: less than 10%.</p>
