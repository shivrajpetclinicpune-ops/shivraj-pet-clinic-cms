<h2>What is High Rise Syndrome?</h2><p>High Rise Syndrome is the pattern of injuries when cats fall from apartment balconies. In Pune's high-rise neighbourhoods — Wakad, Pimple Nilakh, Baner, Aundh — we see multiple cases every month.</p><div class='post-highlight'>⚠️ The paradox: cats falling from 2–6 floors often sustain MORE injuries than from 7+ floors. Above terminal velocity cats relax and spread impact. Shorter falls can be deadlier.</div><h2>Common Injuries</h2><ul><li><strong>Fractured jaw</strong> — the chin hits first</li><li><strong>Pneumothorax</strong> — collapsed lung from chest impact. Needs immediate treatment</li><li><strong>Fractured limbs and pelvis</strong></li><li><strong>Internal organ damage</strong> — liver, spleen, bladder rupture</li><li><strong>Spinal injuries</strong> — paralysis risk</li></ul><h2>What to Do Immediately</h2><ol><li>Stay calm — panic worsens the cat's shock</li><li>Do NOT cradle — possible spinal injury</li><li>Slide cat onto a flat board or box — support whole body</li><li>Keep warm — shock causes temperature drop</li><li>Call us immediately: +91 77569 65169</li></ol><div class='post-warning'>⚠️ Never wait and watch. A cat that walks away may still have internal bleeding. Always get a post-fall examination. We have in-house X-ray and ultrasound for immediate diagnosis.</div><h2>Prevention</h2><p>Install balcony safety netting — ₹1,500–₹4,000 for a standard 2BHK balcony. This single investment prevents years of trauma.</p>
