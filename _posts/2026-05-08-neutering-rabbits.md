<h2>Why Neutering Your Rabbit Matters</h2><p>Uterine adenocarcinoma affects over 80% of unspayed female rabbits by age 5. It is the single most common cause of death in female rabbits.</p><div class='post-highlight'>🏥 At Shivraj Pet Clinic, we perform rabbit neutering using specialised exotic animal gas anaesthesia — one of very few clinics in PCMC qualified to do this safely.</div><h2>Benefits of Neutering</h2><ul><li><strong>Prevents uterine cancer</strong> — eliminates the #1 cause of death in females</li><li><strong>Reduces territorial aggression</strong> — biting, lunging and spraying decrease significantly</li><li><strong>Longer lifespan</strong> — 8–12 years vs 5–6 unneutered</li><li><strong>Better bonding</strong> — calmer, more affectionate, easier to litter-train</li></ul><h2>The Procedure</h2><ol><li>Pre-surgery blood test in our in-house lab</li><li>Gas anaesthesia — gentle, monitored throughout</li><li>Surgery: 20–35 minutes</li><li>Recovery monitoring — closely watched</li><li>Same-day discharge with written post-op care instructions</li></ol><div class='post-warning'>⚠️ <strong>Critical:</strong> Rabbits must NOT be fasted before surgery. Their digestive system must keep moving. This is a dangerous mistake made at non-exotic clinics.</div><h2>Best Age to Neuter</h2><p>Females: 4–6 months. Males: 3.5–5 months. Earlier is always better — cancer risk in females begins at 18 months.</p>
