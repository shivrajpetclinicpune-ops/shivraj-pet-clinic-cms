<h2>Are Food Allergies Common in Dogs?</h2><p>Yes — and widely underdiagnosed. Food allergies account for 10–15% of all allergy cases we see. Symptoms overlap with environmental allergies, making diagnosis a process of careful elimination.</p><div class='post-highlight'>🔍 Key clue: Environmental allergies are seasonal. Food allergies happen <strong>year-round</strong>. If your dog itches in winter — think food first.</div><h2>Signs of a Food Allergy</h2><ul><li><strong>Itching</strong> — especially paws, ears, groin, and belly</li><li><strong>Recurrent ear infections</strong> — diet is often the cause</li><li><strong>Chronic skin issues</strong> — red, flaky, irritated skin year-round</li><li><strong>Digestive issues</strong> — loose stools, vomiting, excessive gas</li></ul><h2>Most Common Allergens</h2><ol><li>Chicken — most common in Indian dogs</li><li>Beef, Dairy, Wheat, Eggs, Soy</li></ol><h2>Diagnosis</h2><p>The gold standard is an <strong>elimination diet trial</strong> — 8–12 weeks feeding a novel protein your dog has never eaten. Blood tests and skin prick tests for food allergies in dogs are unreliable.</p><div class='post-warning'>⚠️ Do not rely on quick test kits sold online. Only the elimination diet trial gives a definitive answer.</div>
