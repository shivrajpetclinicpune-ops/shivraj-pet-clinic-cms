<h2>Why Diet is Everything for Pet Birds</h2><p>70% of illness we see in birds at Shivraj Pet Clinic is directly linked to poor diet. Most Indian pet owners feed their birds only seeds. Seeds are high in fat and low in almost every vital nutrient.</p><div class='post-highlight'>💡 The ideal bird diet: 60–70% pellets, 20–30% fresh vegetables, and only 5–10% seeds as treats.</div><h2>Budgies</h2><ul><li><strong>Pellets:</strong> Harrison's Bird Foods or Zupreem — form the base of the diet</li><li><strong>Vegetables:</strong> Leafy greens, carrots, capsicum</li><li><strong>Fruits:</strong> Apple (no seeds), mango, pomegranate — small amounts</li><li><strong>Avoid:</strong> Avocado (toxic), onion, garlic, chocolate, caffeine</li></ul><h2>Cockatiels</h2><ul><li>Pellet-first approach — rotate vegetables daily</li><li>Cooked egg — excellent protein source</li><li>Calcium: cuttlebone always available in cage</li></ul><h2>African Grey Parrots &amp; Macaws</h2><ul><li>Cooked legumes — lentils, chickpeas (excellent protein)</li><li>Fresh sprouts — nutritional powerhouse</li><li>Macaws: include nuts in moderation — Brazil nuts, almonds</li></ul><div class='post-warning'>⚠️ <strong>Never feed birds:</strong> Avocado, chocolate, onion, garlic, alcohol, caffeine, fruit seeds, or any processed human food. These can be fatal even in small quantities.</div>
