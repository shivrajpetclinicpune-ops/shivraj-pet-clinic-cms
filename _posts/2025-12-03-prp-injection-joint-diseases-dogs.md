<h2>What is PRP Therapy?</h2><p>Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) therapy uses your dog's own blood to heal damaged joints. We draw blood, concentrate the platelets in a centrifuge, and inject directly into the affected joint.</p><div class='post-highlight'>✅ PRP is 100% natural — it uses the patient's own blood. No foreign substances, no steroids, no risk of rejection.</div><h2>What Conditions Does PRP Treat?</h2><ul><li><strong>Osteoarthritis</strong> — most common in Labradors and German Shepherds</li><li><strong>Hip Dysplasia</strong> — abnormal hip joint development</li><li><strong>Elbow Dysplasia</strong> — frequent in large breeds</li><li><strong>Ligament injuries</strong> — partial ACL tears</li></ul><h2>The Procedure</h2><ol><li>Blood draw: 10–20ml from the dog</li><li>Centrifugation: blood spun to concentrate platelets</li><li>Injection into the joint under light sedation</li><li>Same-day discharge with rest instructions</li></ol><h2>What to Expect</h2><ul><li>Mild soreness 24–48 hours — completely normal</li><li>Improvement within 2–4 weeks</li><li>Full benefit at 6–8 weeks, lasting 6–12 months</li></ul>
